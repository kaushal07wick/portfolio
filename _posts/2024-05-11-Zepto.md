## Business

As said by `Aadit Palicha` in an interview with `Nithin Kamath`, indian population are more likely to buy loose groceries such as, atta, rice, dal, and cereals and there have been no such big brands made for a specific grocery item except aashirwaad.

As I have seen and frequently do as well, that I prefer this kind of groceries freshly made, so I buy from a mill or "atta chakki", especially for my parents it gives a sense of pure and genuine material.

Well to crack this it would be hard, but we could stil do one thing:-

* team up with a local big capacity mill
* sort out all the items people prefer 
* and package this items with our, **Zepto** logo on it, which can implicate trust on our brand.
* this is not new, as amazon and flipkart do these on small scale brands, capture the trend and put thier label on it to enchance the trust factor on the them.

and also said the Zepto CEO, indians are frugal and want everything in cheap.

It's 100% true.

so, let's examine what and how they want cheap

* Price of an item should be less than MRP (duhh..)
* while ordering I should pay less than total MRP as well no platform fee or delivery charges, or else what is the use of using this.. I can just go buy from a shop nearby my apartment.

how can we make sure, we fulfill these needs.

We as a business need money to operate and become profitable, so i'll try to justify this with being considerate for the business as well as customers.

* no platform fee and delivery fee is non negotiable, but we can surely make it less,
my idea is to reduce the price, but put delivery fee on all orders irrespective of order size.

* we are maintaining the dark stores, we can track the most heavily used and ordered 50 items (for now) and source it directly from the factory.

* this way it can be cheap and the transportation cost can be adjusted, when we mark it up.

* the delivery hubs, and delivery agents are working in sync, but what I observed that in a small locality, sub 500m radius, a single delivery agent or 2 delivery agents can deliver almost all the products in the `10 -15min` window. But due to order placing time, sometimes multiple delivery agents roam in the same locality. According to me it is not a efficient use of resource.

* we can make use of grouping the delivery agents into centre point, where they can exchange the products going to the same locality, and the other free drivers can fullfill other orders.
